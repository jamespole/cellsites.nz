The above pictures are from a 2007 visit. The pictures below are from a 2015 visit, coincidentally on the same date and month as the 2007 visit! It is very interesting to see the impact of site sharing. The 2007 picture shows each operator having their own separate set of antennas: Orange, T-Mobile and Vodafone (and possibly also Three). Jump forward to 2015 and there are now only two sets of antennas. One set is for the Mobile Broadband Network Limited (MBNL) joint venture between EE (itself formed from the merger of Orange and T-Mobile) and Three. The other set is for the Cornerstone joint venture between O2 and Vodafone. This seems to be a much better arrangement.
